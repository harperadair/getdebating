For schools & teachers

Three ways to bring debating into your school.

Every school and group is different, so our work takes a few different shapes — from a single workshop to a full year of coaching.

Bring Get Debating to your school
Strand 01

In-school workshops

Short, high-energy sessions that introduce argument structure, public speaking, and rebuttal — a good starting point for a school that's new to debating, or wants a one-off boost.

Best for

Schools new to debating

No existing debating society required — we bring the structure and the energy.

Format

Single or short series of sessions

Delivered in-class or as part of a wider event, with a hands-on, practise-heavy approach.

Typical outcome

A confident first step

Students leave able to structure a basic argument and speak on their feet.

Strand 02

Long-term debate coaching

A sustained programme across a term or year, with competition preparation and regular feedback — for schools building or growing a debating team.

Best for

Existing or growing debating societies

Schools that want to compete, or deepen a team that's already speaking.

Format

Regular sessions over a term or year

Structured practice, motion preparation, and one-to-one feedback on speeches.

Typical outcome

Competition-ready speakers

Students build the skills and confidence to represent their school at competitions.

Strand 03

Debate groups & student programmes

Small-group coaching for students outside a single school, including mixed-school sessions — a good fit for students whose school doesn't currently offer debating.

Best for

Individual students & small groups

Including students whose school has no debating society of its own.

Format

Small-group sessions, mixed-school

Students from different schools train and practise together.

Typical outcome

A debating community outside the classroom

Students build skills and connect with other young debaters across Dublin.

Before you ask

Questions teachers usually have

Will this add to my workload?

We plan and run the sessions. A teacher's role is typically light supervision and coordination with us — not preparing or delivering content yourself.

Is this only for strong or "elite" students?

No — our workshops are designed as an accessible starting point for students with no debating experience, not just for an existing debating team. Long-term coaching can build toward competition, but the door is open regardless of starting level.

What about supervision and safeguarding?

All coaches who work directly with students are Garda vetted, and sessions run with the knowledge of school staff. See our safeguarding & child protection statement for full detail.

Will this crowd out our existing timetable?

Workshops can run as a one-off during class time or as part of a wider event; long-term coaching typically runs after school, outside timetabled hours. We'll agree a format that fits your school before starting.
